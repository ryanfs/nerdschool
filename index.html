Start Here

You’ve landed here because you want to learn to code. Maybe having this new skill will lead to a new job. Maybe you want a new job because you think a coding job will be more intellectually stimulating than your current job. Maybe you think you’ll make more money. Maybe you plain don’t like your old job or its prospects for the future and you want a change. Maybe I should stop taking broad guesses about your life. Whatever the reason, we’re here to help guide you so you don't fail.

Who am I?

I am the person I just described above. I wanted a change and started off by learning the basics on my own. Learning the basics wasn't so bad, but eventually I hit a wall. I knew I could do it, but just needed some guidance for how to proceed, and a curriculum would have been nice, and personalized tutoring would be even nicer. I had fundamental and conceptual questions that stackoverflow never seemed to quite answer clearly enough.

I needed a different approach if I was really going to learn this stuff, so I up and left my job to attend a coding bootcamp for three months. Having an actual teacher answer my questions was exactly what I needed to get through that wall.

Why now?

You’re like me. You’ve tried telling yourself that you’ll learn on your own, but unless something changes, this process of "learning to code" will drag on indefinitely. It doesn’t have to be that way. You will get to a point (after you’ve built up enough fundamental knowledge, for me it was data structures) where the training wheels come off and you can navigate problems on your own. Your only constraints are going to be how fast you can type, and more importantly how well you can structure your programs.

I’m here to get you over your largest hurdles so you can start having those magical "aha moments" more often.

Who are you?

You have a vision for where you want to be in one year from now. You already know the basics because you've done online tutorials but now you want to get serious. You're aware there are tons of free resources out there, but just don't know which ones to start with.

You want to write scripts to automate your job, or you want to be able to build your own software without relying on some magical (expensive) person to come along and build it for you, or you seriously want to transition to a career in software engineering.

How does it work?

You'll get 8 weeks of personal 1 on 1 tutoring and lifetime access to your curriculum and materials.

Application

I want to get to know you and your motivations for wanting to join the program. After filling out the application, you’ll get a response within 24 hours.

Orientation

I need to know your current level, and what your goals are in order to personalize your program. We'll do an orientation to get to know each other over google hangouts so I can get a sense of what you’re looking for.

Assignments

You’ll have weekly two part assignments: reading, and coding.The reading will introduce you to concepts at your own pace, and then in our tutoring session we can go over anything that may not have made sense the first time around. The assignments give you practice coding out solutions to those concepts.

Tutoring and Code Review

We’ll have code reviews for all of your assignments. So even if you get your program execute return the right answer, I’ll teach you all of the various ways you can get something to work, the pros and cons of those techniques, and what the “most correct” or most elegant solution looks like at any point in time. What you'll learn in a code review is important to writing professional code, but more importantly, you'll learn how to give a code review which is equally important in a professional environment.

Fill out the application (less than 5 minutes) to see if we are both a good fit for each other.
